Areas of growth and complexity
1
SSP ALLOCATION
Enterprise agreements bundling hardware and services demand precise SSP allocation at every deal close.
Axon's enterprise agreements cover devices, software, cloud services, and SOW-based professional services sold together under a single contract. Allocating the transaction price across each distinct performance obligation requires defensible SSP analysis — at Axon's volume, doing that manually creates material audit risk and close-cycle drag.
2
CONTRACT MODIFICATIONS
Written change orders that adjust fees or scope trigger reallocation events that can't be handled in a spreadsheet at scale.
Axon's enterprise terms require written change orders whenever scope, fees, or schedule are modified. Each modification is a potential ASC 606 reallocation event — prospective or cumulative catch-up — and with hundreds of active enterprise agreements, finance teams are forced to manually re-examine and repost entries that should be automated.
3
BUNDLING
Named platform plans like the AI Era Plan and Officer Safety Plan create multi-element bundling complexity that compounds with every new AI product added.
Axon's AI Era Plan and Officer Safety Plan bundle cameras, TASER devices, cloud software, AI features, and support into a single tailored sales motion. The April 2026 launch of Axon Vision, Axon Assistant, and Axon 911 adds new distinct performance obligations to existing plan structures — each requiring identification, SSP assignment, and ongoing recognition tracking without manual intervention.
