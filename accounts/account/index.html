Areas of growth and complexity
1
SSP ALLOCATION
Allocating revenue across bundled AI products sold at a single annual price creates material ASC 606 complexity.
Axon's AI Era Plan packages multiple distinct AI products and features — including Draft One, Real Time Operations, and FUSUS integrations — under one fixed annual enterprise cost. Each element requires its own standalone selling price to allocate transaction price correctly under ASC 606, and those SSPs must be defensible to auditors across thousands of enterprise contracts.
2
BUNDLING
Interoperable hardware, sensors, software, and AI sold as a unified suite requires precise de-bundling for compliant recognition.
Axon markets enterprise solutions as fully integrated suites — body cameras, TASER devices, Fleet sensors, cloud software, and AI capabilities are positioned as one connected system. When contracts combine hardware deliverables with multi-year SaaS and AI subscriptions, each performance obligation must be identified, valued, and recognized on its own schedule, creating compounding manual work at scale.
3
EVENT-BASED RECOGNITION
The Technology Assurance Plan triggers revenue events at device upgrades, accessory additions, and mid-cycle contract changes.
TAP combines recurring subscription payments with scheduled hardware refresh cycles and variable charges for accessories, added services, or device swaps. Each upgrade delivery, accessory shipment, or contract modification is a discrete revenue event that must be recognized at the right point in time or over the right period — without waiting for month-end to catch up on what was triggered during the quarter.
