OPEN REV REC JOBS
Hiring a Revenue Recognition Director signals that current ASC 606 processes can't scale to the post-acquisition complexity.
The newly posted Revenue Recognition Director role (May 29, 2026) is explicitly scoped to lead ASC 606 compliance across software, semiconductor IP, and services contracts inherited through the Synopsys acquisition of ANSYS. The Director will own the revenue close, prepare quarterly packages for Big 4 auditors, and drive automation across the quote-to-cash cycle — a clear sign that today's manual processes aren't built for the combined entity's transaction volume and contract complexity.
2
ERP MIGRATION
An ERP upgrade mid-acquisition integration creates a dangerous window where revenue accounting can break or get rebuilt incorrectly.
The same job posting calls out "a major ERP upgrade" happening in parallel with the ANSYS acquisition integration and the build-out of an offshore center of excellence. ERP migrations routinely expose gaps in revenue recognition logic — particularly for complex license types and consumption-based contracts — that were previously masked by manual workarounds. Getting the rev rec layer right before the ERP cutover prevents months of cleanup and audit exposure.
3
USAGE & CONSUMPTION
Elastic pay-per-use and HPC session-based licensing create event-driven recognition obligations that straight-line ERP logic can't handle.
ANSYS's Elastic licensing model charges customers per analysis run based on computing capacity — cores, GCUs, and GPUs consumed per solver or HPC session. Each checkout event triggers a recognition obligation distinct from annual or perpetual license schedules. Across a growing portfolio of simulation products and cloud hardware, these high-volume metered events must be captured, allocated under ASC 606, and tied back to contracts in real time — not reconciled manually at month-end.
